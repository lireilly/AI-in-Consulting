Module 1

The Consulting Landscape Is Shifting

~4 min

Most people looking at AI and consulting feel one of two things: fear (will AI take my job?) or curiosity (how do I actually use AI to get hired?). This workshop starts with the second question — because it's the one that will make your resume stand out.

🤔
Before we start — quick reflection: Have you ever worried that AI might make consulting jobs obsolete? And have you thought about how AI might actually help you get hired?

The reality is more nuanced than headlines suggest. AI is not eliminating consulting — it is changing what consulting demands. Firms are shifting from hiring people who can process data quickly to hiring people who can think critically about what the data means, what it misses, and what to do about it.

That shift benefits you — if you prepare for it deliberately. The rest of this workshop will help you do exactly that.

Module 3

Skills That Still Matter (and That AI Can't Replicate)

~4 min

So what does McKinsey's shift — and the broader consulting industry's move toward AI — actually mean for the skills you need to develop? Based on how top firms are now structuring their work, six capabilities have emerged as the ones that distinguish high-performing candidates.

✍️
Effective prompting & question formulation
Getting useful output from AI starts with asking the right questions — a skill that mirrors good consulting communication.
🔍
Critical evaluation of AI outputs
AI can be confidently wrong. Recognizing gaps, biases, and unsupported claims in AI responses is now a core consulting skill.
⚖️
Knowing when to trust vs. challenge AI recommendations
Not every AI recommendation is wrong — but knowing when to push back is what separates great analysts from good ones.
💡
Novel approaches & fresh perspectives
AI recombines existing knowledge; it rarely generates genuinely new strategic framings. That's still a human advantage.
🤝
Understanding client-specific requirements
AI doesn't know your client's culture, politics, or unspoken constraints. Context-gathering from humans is irreplaceable.
🚀
Translating AI insights into actionable recommendations
An AI can surface patterns; a consultant turns those patterns into a strategy a client can actually implement.

Case Study: Talbot Trucks & the EV Decision

~8 min

Now let's apply everything to a realistic consulting case. Read the brief below, then work through three steps: prompt the AI, validate the outputs, and synthesize your recommendation.

📋
Case Brief — Talbot Trucks: Talbot Trucks is a European commercial truck manufacturer evaluating whether to begin manufacturing electric trucks (eTrucks) to reduce its carbon footprint. They need to determine if this investment makes sense.
Customers Large trucking companies + small owner-operators
Current Technology Diesel engines — fast refueling at existing gas stations
New Technology eTrucks — e-motors, batteries, slower charging infrastructure
Core Challenge Transition is disruptive for both the manufacturer AND the customers

Step 1 — Prompt the AI

Write at least two prompts you would use to gather AI-assisted information to help Talbot Trucks make this decision. Try to move from a broad framing prompt to a more targeted follow-up.

0 characters

Step 2 — Validate the AI Output

Imagine the AI returned results. Before using them, you need to critically examine what it gave you. Work through these three validation moves:

  1. Validate reliability — Ask yourself: "What information seems reliable? What needs external verification?" Think about data sources, recency, and AI hallucination risks.
  2. Identify gaps — Ask: "What did the AI miss? What client-specific context isn't captured here?" For Talbot, this might include European regulatory specifics, fleet contract structures, or union considerations.
  3. Challenge assumptions — The AI might say "eTrucks have lower maintenance costs" — but does that hold for battery replacement after 300,000 km? What about charging infrastructure in rural Poland or Northern Scandinavia?

Step 3 — Synthesize Your Recommendation

Now pull everything together. A consulting recommendation is not "the AI said X." It is your structured judgment — informed by the AI's research, your gap analysis, and your own reasoning.
